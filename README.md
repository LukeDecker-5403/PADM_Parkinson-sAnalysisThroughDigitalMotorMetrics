# PADM_Parkinson-sAnalysisThroughDigitalMotorMetrics
PADM (Parkinson’s Analysis through Digital Motor metrics) is a research project focused on detecting early signs of Parkinson’s disease through subtle keyboard and touchpad interactions. By analyzing fine motor and cognitive patterns, PADM aims to create a non-invasive, accessible tool for early screening and continuous data-driven improvement.

Proposal:

“Success isn’t something that happens by chance; it’s built through the opportunity one creates for oneself.” is something my grandfather told me throughout my life. My goal with PADM (Parkinson’s Analysis Through Digital Motor Metrics) is to provide a program focused on detecting early signs of Parkinson’s Disease through subtle keyboard and touchpad interactions. This research will allow me to apply machine learning tools to practical problems and conduct in-depth data analysis to draw meaningful results. The tools I plan to use will primarily be drawn from my coursework in Intro to Artificial Intelligence and Information Retrieval, where I developed practical experience with machine learning and advanced data analysis methods. As for how this project affects my future, it will help prepare me for classes in my upcoming senior year, and provide me with real-world applications I can carry into my professional career. Annually, 60,000 people in the United States are diagnosed with Parkinson’s disease, and approximately 1,000,000 individuals are currently living with it. With the average age of diagnosis being 60 years old, my personal mission is to contribute to earlier detection methods that can improve quality of life, expand treatment opportunities, and ultimately provide individuals and families with more time and clarity in confronting the disease. 

Referenced in the introduction above, PADM’s goal is to provide non-invasive early detection methodologies by analyzing fine motor skills and cognitive functionality. This project will combine both research and creative development, as I build and refine the program’s backend until I am confident enough in the data and results to expand it into a full application over the summer.
The program will begin by prompting the user for demographic and background information, including age, sex, handedness, overall sleep quality, and genetic predisposition to Parkinson’s disease. It will then guide the user through a series of motor skill assessments, measuring tapping speed in each hand, dominant and non-dominant tremor rates (in Hz), and tracing precision tasks performed through a keyboard and touchpad interface.

Following the motor assessments, the program will administer a set of cognitive aptitude tests designed to evaluate memory retention and processing speed. The collected data will then be analyzed and compared against reference data obtained from the Michael J. Fox Foundation, ultimately generating a similarity score based on observed patterns. The primary purpose of this program is not to serve as a diagnostic tool, but to provide comparative, data-driven insight aligned with established research patterns.
